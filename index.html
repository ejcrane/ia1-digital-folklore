we're creating the web, putting whatever we want and saying whatever we want. we're borrowing from this early ethos of expressing ourselves.

the website is a powerful and unwieldy thing. you can say anything you want in the exact way you want to, but doing so takes time and effort. back when the internet was a veritable wilderness, cutting through wires and forging one's own path was the only way to make a website, and these paths had their own intricacies that made each website admittedly not that great, but unique nonetheless.

nowadays, the easiest paths to proclaimed self-expression are six-lane superhighways, fresh asphalt poured by a big few companies, namely meta, google, amazon, and others. it is far easier to set up a profile on instagram than to make and host your own website. but these roads have speed limits, they have rules; these roads have terms and conditions that bound user expression. the roads have different names and different colors, yet they all feel the same. tyranny of the majority dictated what was considered universally appealing, and appealed to the masses the corporations did.

the cost? self-expression. INDIVIDUALITY! the profiles may have different photos, but they all feel the same. this is why i prefer the website to the profile.
